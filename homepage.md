## Problem
**Farmers markets rely on foot traffic, weather, and hope.**
Local food competes with national supply chains built on logistics, software and data. Most local commerce still runs on spreadsheets, foot traffic and hope. Communities deserve infrastructure that matches the scale of the opportunity.

## The Shift
**Local commerce needs infrastructure, not enthusiasm.**
Digital coordination must support physical markets. We are shifting the model from weather-dependent events to predictable, data-driven marketplaces powered by pre-orders and automation. Local food wins when convenience, trust, and selection converge. REKO makes that convergence operational.

## How REKO Works (System Overview)
**The operating layer for local food.**
1. **Discover:** Consumers explore producers, products and seasonal availability in one unified marketplace. Local supply becomes visible and searchable, not scattered.

2. **Pre-Order:** Demand shifts earlier in the week. Orders are placed before production peaks, giving producers clarity instead of guesswork.

3. **Aggregate:** REKO coordinates orders across producers and locations, organizing inventory, fulfillment and timing into a single operational layer.

4. **Fulfill:** Markets, pickup hubs or local delivery. The system adapts to the format that best serves the community. One engine, multiple models.

5. **Analyze:** Real-time data reveals what sells, who buys, how often and at what depth. Markets and producers gain visibility they’ve never had before.

6. **Adapt:** Using data and AI, REKO continuously optimizes timing, assortment and engagement. The system learns from local behavior and evolves with the community.

7. **Built for Modern Local Systems:** Unlike legacy tools or one-off market software, REKO uses data and AI to optimize inventory, demand timing and fulfillment models. It adapts to how your community buys, not the other way around.

Key Metrics
MOS. Median Order Size
Definition: Median dollar value of a single order


Unit of analysis: Order


What it tells us: What a typical order looks like right now. The true center of buying behavior, not skewed by stock-ups or outliers.
OPC. Orders per Consumer (median)
Definition: Median number of orders per consumer in a 28-day window


Unit of analysis: Consumer


What it tells us: Habit strength. Whether ordering has become routine.
PPC. Producers per Consumer (median)
Definition: Median number of distinct producers a consumer purchases from in a 28-day window


Unit of analysis: Consumer


What it tells us: Portfolio breadth. How wide a buyer’s trusted set of producers is.
RPC. Revenue per Consumer (median)
Definition: Median total revenue generated per consumer in a 28-day window


Unit of analysis: Consumer


What it tells us: Economic depth of a typical buyer, independent of power users.
Leveraging Key Metrics for Automated Messaging and Communications
Reko will serve as the central hub for marketing automation. By using the key metrics as signals, we can trigger personalized in‑app messages, emails and other communications.
When PPC dips below a threshold, automated reminders encourage customers to order from other producers. Cross-selling is guided by the cross-sell matrix.
While Reko orchestrates these automated communications centrally, each farmers market retains the flexibility to customize and supplement marketing efforts for their local community, ensuring consistency with the overall strategy while preserving autonomy.
Targets | Who we’re marketing/selling to
1. Producers
Why they care:
Producers want predictable revenue, less waste and less time spent guessing what to bring to market. They already know how to make great food. What they lack is demand certainty and modern tools.
What we say:
REKO turns farmers markets from a gamble into a system. Pre-orders replace hope with data. Top producers earn more without working more hours.
2. Consumers
Why they care:
Consumers are already spending heavily on food. Most of that money leaves the region via shipped boxes and national brands. The friction isn’t values; it’s convenience.
What we say:
REKO gives you the best parts of online grocery shopping without giving up local food. Order ahead. Pick up once. Know your producers. Build a better cart.
Behavioral shift:
We explicitly position REKO as a replacement for shipped food (Walmart, big-box markets) and specialty grocery add-ons. The promise is a larger, more satisfying local cart, not a one-off weekend purchase.
3. Markets
Why they care:
Markets are under pressure to increase vendor success, consumer turnout and relevance beyond Saturday mornings. They need proof that digital tools drive real-world outcomes.
What we say to prospective markets:
REKO grows GMV, not just foot traffic.
Markets using pre-orders see higher average order sizes, more reliable vendor participation and stronger buyer retention. RekoWurks gives market managers visibility they have never had before. What sells. Who sells it. Where growth comes from.
REKO is not software bolted onto a market. It is infrastructure for modern local commerce.

Tactics and Execution
We start local, visible and physical.
Market launches are treated as civic events. Producer spotlights create social proof. “Super-Producers” with social media reach create momentum. Email and SMS drive pre-orders before market day. On-site signage reinforces the digital loop. Analytics are shared back with markets and producers to reinforce value.
Every tactic points to the same outcome.
Bigger carts. More predictable sales. A better local food shopping experience.
